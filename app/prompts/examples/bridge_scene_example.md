# Bridge scene example

## Context given to Gemini
- Character: Kai (junior developer)
- Concept: database normalisation
- Scene type: bridge (introductory, no heavy pushback)
- Setting: campus café

## Expected output

[narration] The café is quieter than usual. A laptop screen glows at the corner table, surrounded by empty coffee cups.

[character:Kai] *neutral* Hey, you're in the Digital Tech class right? Mind if I ask you something?

[character:Kai] *concerned* I've been building this app for tracking our club's equipment loans. But every time someone borrows something, I'm copying their full name, email, and phone number into the loans table.

[character:Kai] *thoughtful* It works... but something feels wrong about it. Like I'm writing the same stuff over and over.

[player_prompt] Kai is describing a database with repeated student details in every loan record. What might be the problem with this design?

[character:Kai] *curious* Hm, interesting. So you think there's a better way to organise this?

[narration] Kai turns the laptop toward you, showing a spreadsheet with dozens of rows — the same three phone numbers appearing over and over.

[character:Kai] *amused* Yeah... it's kind of a mess. Maybe we should look at this more carefully next time.

[narration] The scene ends. A new entry appears in your journal.
