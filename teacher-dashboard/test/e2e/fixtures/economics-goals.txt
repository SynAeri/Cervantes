Economics Assessment Brief

Learning goals:
- Identify key economic indicators including GDP, unemployment, and inflation.
- Describe the impact of government intervention on markets and economic outcomes.
- Evaluate labour management strategies in business and policy contexts.

Assessment requirements:
- Students should compare GDP, CPI, and unemployment in realistic scenarios.
- Students should explain advantages and trade-offs of government policies.
- Students should reflect on business decisions that affect labour productivity, staffing, and market outcomes.

Common misconceptions to test:
- GDP growth alone means everyone is better off.
- Inflation and unemployment move independently of broader economic conditions.
- Government intervention is always harmful to markets.
- Labour management is only about cutting costs rather than balancing productivity, welfare, and long-term growth.
